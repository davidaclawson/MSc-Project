Hybrid Cybersecurity Framework

Below we can see an easy-to-understand table showcasing the requisites set forth by the hybrid cybersecurity framework to ensure a quality level of cybersecurity hygiene.


This framework was developed with the intent of being used by specifically by football clubs and other sporting organisations alike, and developed to be easily understood by someone who does not have a background in Information Technology. Combining 4 already existing cybersecurity frameworks, this tool is incredibly valuable to any of the relevant parties when formulating a robust cybersecurity strategy.


Users can undertake their own gap analysis using the below hybrid cybersecurity strategy by clicking the following link: Gap Analysis Tool


The design of the hybrid cybersecurity framework is as follows:



Category Reference Original Framework Requisite Definition Importance
Identify I1 NIST Inventory of assets. Allows for a business to keep record and monitor all assets under their umbrella. High
Identify I2 NIST Understanding of business role, and critical functions that support operations. Allows for business employees to understand what the business does and what it aims to do. Medium
Identify I3 NIST Identify stakeholders and roles with regards to cybersecurity. Allows employees to understand their roles within the business. Medium
Identify I4 CIS Establish cybersecurity policies. Ensures businesses and their employees are aware of policies that must be adhered to. High
Identify I5 CIS Define responsibilities for personnel with regards to cybersecurity. Ensures relevant personnel are aware of what is expected of them. Medium
Identify I6 CIS Periodic reviews of policies and procedures. Ensures policies and procedures are up-to-date and industry standard. Medium

Protect P1 CIS Implement role-based access controls. Ensures only relevant personnel can access the necessary areas. High
Protect P2 NCSC Implement two-factor authentication system. Ensures a further layer of protection covering assets. High
Protect P3 NCSC Implement encryption on necessary assets. Ensures a further layer of protection covering assets. High
Protect P4 NCSC Periodic reviews of access controls. Ensures personnel who no longer need access to certain areas can no longer do so, and vice versa Medium
Protect P5 NIST Implement data encryption. Ensures data and information is protected and secure. High
Protect P6 NIST Implement data handling policies. Ensures personnel know how to handle data and what to do with it. Medium
Protect P7 CIS Periodic backing up of data. Ensures data is protected if a detrimental action were to occur. Medium
Protect P8 NCSC Regular cybersecurity training for all staff members. Ensures personnel are aware of cybersecurity risks and what is expected of them. Medium
Protect P9 NIST Implement VPN software. Ensures devices are protected when used on foreign networks. High
Protect P10 NIST Implement device protection software, including firewalls and anti-malware software. Ensures devices are protected from malicious acts from occurring. High
Protect P11 NIST Periodic updates of device protection software. Ensures devices are protected from new and modern risks. High
Protect P12 NCSC Secure disposing of devices and data that are no longer required. Ensures data and devices do not fall into the wrong hands. Medium

Detect D1 SQ Implement systems to monitor traffic on network systems and user systems. Helps to track suspicious activity, including identifying the source. High
Detect D2 SQ Implement SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) systems to correlate logs. Helps to prevent security threats from exploited vulnerabilities. Medium
Detect D3 CIS Develop detection procedures. Helps personnel to understand their responsibilities when an anomaly is detected. Medium
Detect D4 CIS Periodic exposure assessments. Helps to expose any vulnerabilities and fixes them. Medium
Detect D5 CIS Periodic penetration testing. Helps to expose any vulnerabilities and fixes them. Medium
Detect D6 CIS Implement anomaly detection software. Helps to expose any anomalies that may breach the network. Medium

Respond RS1 SQ Creation of an incident response plan. Ensures personnel are aware of what to do during an incident. High
Respond RS2 SQ Definition of roles and responsibilities during an incident. Ensures personnel are aware of their own responsibilities during an incident. Medium
Respond RS3 NCSC Implement containment and eradication processes. Processes to ensure incidents are isolated and eradicated to continue protection. Medium
Respond RS4 NCSC Implement software to isolate issues. Ensures issues do not spread to other areas of the business. Medium
Respond RS5 SQ Creation of communication routes for stakeholders during incidents. Ensures stakeholders are aware of incidents occurring and steps taken to remediate. Medium
Respond RS6 SQ Creation of templates to be utilised as status indicators. Ensures continuous updates are given when dealing with incidents. Low

Recover RC1 NIST Creation of recovery plan to establish systems and data. Ensures steps are in place to resume business activity. High
Recover RC2 NIST Periodic testing of recovery processes. Ensures recovery processes are working and up to date. Medium
Recover RC3 NIST Analysis of reports after incidents occur to understand problems. Allows relevant personnel to gain an insight into the problems that have occurred. High
Recover RC4 NIST Update policies based on the analysis of reports. Ensures the best protection is given to the organisation. High
Recover RC5 SQ Communication of recovery steps to stakeholders. Ensures stakeholders are aware of the steps being taken to remediate problems. Medium
